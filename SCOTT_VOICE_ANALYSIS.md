## VOICE CHARACTERISTICS

### Tone
- **Professional but accessible** - not academic, not casual
- **Analytical** - explains WHY things matter, not just WHAT
- **Policy-aware** - governance, regulation, societal impact
- **Balanced** - acknowledges both opportunities AND concerns
- **Business-focused** - economic implications matter
- **Nuanced** - doesn't sensationalize
- **Uses hedging** - "could", "may", "highlights", "risks"
- **Connects dots** - shows relationships between stories

## CONTENT SELECTION CRITERIA

### High Priority
- Government/policy announcements (especially Canadian)
- Canadian angle/implications
- Governance, ethics, safety concerns
- Workforce/economic implications
- Research papers with real insights
- Practical tools with Canadian relevance

### Always Include
- At least 1 Canadian government story
- At least 1 governance/regulation story
- At least 1 positive/bright spot story
- Mix of concerns and opportunities
- Data/specific numbers when available

### Avoid
- Pure hype announcements
- Generic AI news without context
- Stories without Canadian or business angle
- Clickbait headlines

## SUMMARY FORMAT (Critical)

### NOT This:
"AI safety features can be bypassed with poetry."

### DO This:
"Researchers at Italy's Icaro Lab and ethical AI company DexAI discovered that poetry's unpredictable linguistic structure can bypass AI safety guardrails, causing large language models (LLMs) to produce harmful content despite being trained to avoid it. Testing 20 poems on 25 models from nine companies revealed 62% of prompts elicited unsafe responses, including instructions on weapons or hate speech. Some models like OpenAI's GPT-5 nano fared well, whereas Google's Gemini 2.5 pro responded with harmful content 100% of the time. The study highlights a major vulnerability in AI safety systems and calls for improved evaluation methods."

**Key Elements:**
- Who did the research?
- What did they find? (with numbers)
- What does it mean? (implications)
- Why should we care?

## SECTION GUIDELINES

### Headline Summary (8-10 stories)
- Mix of 60% governance/policy + 40% capability/research
- Each story gets 2-3 paragraphs
- Always includes "why it matters" for Canada
- Links provided for each story

### Bright Spot of the Week
- Counterbalance to concerns
- 2-3 positive or impactful stories
- Medical breakthroughs, innovation examples
- Shows balanced perspective

### Tool of the Week
- 1 new tool or capability
- Brief but substantive description
- Link to try it or learn more

### Learning
- Upcoming events, webinars, courses
- Government training opportunities
- Professional development

### Deep Dive
- 4-5 longer, research-heavy pieces
- Academic papers, policy reports
- 3-4 paragraph deep analysis
- "What this means for Canada" angle

### Grain Quality (Optional)
- Applied AI in grain/agriculture
- Research papers on quality control
- Practical farming applications

## WRITING RULES

1. **Always answer "So what?"**
   - Don't just report, explain implications
   - Who is affected?
   - What should they do?

2. **Use specific data**
   - Numbers, percentages, studies
   - Concrete examples, not abstractions

3. **Connect to Canadian context**
   - Almost every story should have a Canadian angle
   - Or why it matters to Canadian policy/business

4. **Balance perspectives**
   - Acknowledge both hype and concerns
   - Don't be doom-and-gloom or Polyanna-ish

5. **Keep paragraphs concise**
   - 2-3 sentences per paragraph
   - Readable in email format

6. **Use bullet points for lists**
   - Easier to scan in email

7. **Always provide links**
   - "Read more" for each headline
   - Sources for deep dives

## METRICS FROM ANALYSIS

- **Average headlines per newsletter:** 8-10
- **Average summary paragraphs per headline:** 2-3
- **Sections per newsletter:** 5-7
- **Avg total stories per newsletter:** 20-25
- **Email length:** ~2000-3000 words (2-4 pages when printed)
- **Publication day:** Thursdays
- **Publication time:** Morning (9-10 AM)

## SENTIMENT DISTRIBUTION

- **Concerns/Risks:** 40% (job loss, security, privacy, regulation concerns)
- **Opportunities:** 35% (tools, breakthroughs, policy innovations)
- **Neutral analysis:** 25% (research, technical deep dives)

Always include uplifting "Bright Spot" section to avoid doom-heavy tone.
